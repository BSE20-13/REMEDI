question 1. DEPRESSED MOOD (sadness, hopeless, helpless, worthless)
answer 1.1. Absent
answer 1.2. These feeling states indicated only on questioning.
answer 1.3. These feeling states spontaneously reported verbally
answer 1.4. Communicates feeling states non-verbally, i.e. through facial expression, posture, voice and tendency to weep.
answer 1.5. User reports virtually only these feeling states in his/her spontaneous verbal and non-verbal communication.

question 2. FEELINGS OF GUILT
answer 2.1. Absent.
answer 2.2. Self reproach, feels he/she has let people down
answer 2.3. Ideas of guilt or rumination over past errors or sinful deeds.
answer 2.4. Present illness is a punishment. Delusions of guilt.
answer 2.5. Hears accusatory or denunciatory voices and/or experiences threatening visual hallucinations.

question 3. SUICIDE
answer 3.1. Absent.
answer 3.2. Feels life is not worth living
answer 3.3. Wishes he/she were dead or any thoughts of possible death to self.
answer 3.4. Ideas or gestures of suicide.
answer 3.5. Attempts at suicide

question 4. INSOMNIA: EARLY IN THE NIGHT
answer 4.1. No difficulty falling asleep.
answer 4.2. Complains of occasional difficulty falling asleep, i.e. more than 1/2 hour
answer 4.3. Complains of nightly difficulty falling asleep.

question 5. INSOMNIA: MIDDLE OF THE NIGHT
answer 5.1. No difficulty.
answer 5.2. User complains of being restless and disturbed during the night.
answer 5.3. Waking during the night - any getting out of bed rates 2 (except for purposes of voiding)

question 6. INSOMNIA: EARLY HOURS OF THE MORNING
answer 6.1. No difficulty
answer 6.2. Waking in early hours of the morning but goes back to sleep.
answer 6.3. Unable to fall asleep again if he/she gets out of bed.

question7. WORK AND ACTIVITIES
answer 7.1.  No difficulty.
answer 7.7.  Thoughts and feelings of incapacity, fatigue or weakness related to activities, work or hobbies.
answer 7.3.  Loss of interest in activity, hobbies or work - either directly reported by the patient or indirect in listlessness, indecision and vacillation (feels he/she has to push self to work or activities).
answer 7.3.  Decrease in actual time spent in activities or decrease in productivity. Rate 3 if the patient does not spend at least three hours a day in activities (job or hobbies) excluding routine chores.
answer 7.4.  Stopped working because of present illness. Rate 4 if user engages in no activities except routine chores,or if patient fails to perform routine chores unassisted.

question 8. RETARDATION (slowness of thought and speech, impaired ability to concentrate, decreased motor activity)
answer 8.1. Normal speech and thought.
answer 8.2. Slight retardation during the interview.
answer 8.3. Obvious retardation during the interview.
answer 8.4. Interview difficult.
answer 8.5. Complete stupor.

question 9. AGITATION
answer 9.1. None.
answer 9.2. Fidgetiness.
answer 9.3. Playing with hands, hair, etc.
answer 9.4. Moving about, can't sit still.
answer 9.5. Hand wringing, nail biting, hair-pulling, biting of lips.

question 10. ANXIETY PSYCHIC
answer 10.1. No difficulty.
answer 10.2. Subjective tension and irritability.
answer 10.3. Worrying about minor matters.
answer 10.4. Apprehensive attitude apparent in face or speech.
answer 10.5. Fears expressed without questioning

question 11. ANXIETY SOMATIC (physiological concomitants of anxiety) such as: gastro-intestinal - dry mouth, wind, indigestion, diarrhea, cramps, belching cardio-vascular - palpitations, headaches respiratory - hyperventilation, sighing urinary frequency sweating
answer 11.1. Absent.
answer 11.2. Mild.
answer 11.3. Moderate.
answer 11.4. Severe.
answer 11.5. Incapacitating.

question 12. SOMATIC SYMPTOMS GASTRO-INTESTINAL
answer 12.1. None.
answer 12.2. Loss of appetite but eating without staff encouragement. Heavy feelings in abdomen.
answer 12.3. Difficulty eating without staff urging. Requests or requires laxatives or medication for bowels or medication for gastro-intestinal symptoms.

question 13. GENERAL SOMATIC SYMPTOMS
answer 13.1. None.
answer 13.2. Heaviness in limbs, back or head. Backaches, headaches, muscle aches. Loss of energy and fatigability.
answer 13.3. Any clear-cut symptom rates 2.

question 14. GENITAL SYMPTOMS (symptoms such as loss of libido,menstrual disturbances)
answer 14.1. Absent.
answer 14.2. Mild.
answer 14.3. Severe.

question 15. HYPOCHONDRIASIS
answer 15.1. Not present.
answer 15.2. Self-absorption (bodily).
answer 15.3. Preoccupation with health.
answer 15.4. Frequent complaints, requests for help, etc.
answer 15.5. Hypochondriacal delusions.

question 16. LOSS OF WEIGHT
answer 16.1. No weight loss.
answer 16.2. Probable weight present illness.
answer 16.3. Definite (according to patient) weight in week.
answer 16.4. Not assessed.

question 17. INSIGHT
answer 17.1. Acknowledges being depressed and ill.
answer 17.2. Acknowledges illness but attributes cause to bad food,climate, overwork, virus, need for rest, etc.
answer 17.3. Denies being ill at all.
